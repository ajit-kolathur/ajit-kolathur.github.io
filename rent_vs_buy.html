Explanation of Formulae

The Mortgage Payment is calculated as: \( M = \frac{L \cdot i (1+i)^n}{(1+i)^n - 1} \), where \( L = P (1-d) \) is the loan amount, \( i = \frac{r}{12} \) is the monthly interest rate (with \( r \) as the annual rate), and \( n = 12 \times \text{Loan Term} \).

The Remaining Balance after \( T \) years is: \( L(T) = L \cdot \frac{(1+i)^n - (1+i)^{12T}}{(1+i)^n - 1} \).

Thus, the Buying Net Equity is: \( E_{buy} = P - L(T) \).

For Renting, the initial down payment \( D = P \cdot d \) is invested, and if the total monthly cost of buying (mortgage payment plus HOA) exceeds the rent \( R \), the difference \( C = \max(M + \mathrm{HOA} - R,\,0) \) is also invested monthly. With monthly compounding, the future value after \( T \) years is: \[ FV = D\,(1+\tfrac{r_{stock}}{12})^{12T} + C\,\frac{(1+\tfrac{r_{stock}}{12})^{12T}-1}{\tfrac{r_{stock}}{12}}\,. \]

Finally, the Net Asset Difference is: \( \Delta E = E_{buy} - FV \).
